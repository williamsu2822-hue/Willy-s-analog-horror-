ARCHIVE ROOM // CAM 01 REC: 23:07 // NO DATE

You are alone in the archive room. A stack of unlabeled VHS tapes sits beside an old CRT monitor. The screen hums with a low buzz, faint static crawling at the edges. On the desk, someone has written in marker:

“DO NOT WATCH ALONE. DO NOT WATCH OUT OF ORDER.”

Two tapes are already halfway out of their cases: one marked A – HOUSE, the other B – SERVICE TUNNEL.

TAPE A // CUT 01 CAM 02 // DRIVEWAY

The recording flickers into life. A single-story house sits at the end of a quiet road. The porch light is on, but the windows are dark. A car idles in the driveway. The headlights are on. No one is inside.

The timer on the screen keeps jumping backward by exactly three seconds.

TAPE A // CUT 03 CAM 03 // DRIVER SIDE

The view jumps closer with a harsh click. The driver’s seat is empty. The engine is running. On the passenger seat lies a single VHS tape with a handwritten label:

“DO NOT FAST-FORWARD.”

As you lean toward the monitor, you notice a reflection in the car window. The archive room. Your chair. Your monitor. But the timecode doesn’t match the clock in here.

TAPE A // CUT 04 CAM 04 // FRONT DOOR

The perspective jumps to the front door. The porch light buzzes faintly. A doormat reads “WELCOME BACK”. The door is closed, but the peephole is glowing with a soft, wrong color, like light coming from an underwater TV.

For a single frame, the text on the doormat changes: “WELCOME INSIDE.” Then it’s normal again.

TAPE B // CUT 01 CAM 07 // ACCESS TUNNEL

The picture snaps to a narrow concrete tunnel, lit by flickering fluorescent lights. A painted arrow on the floor points deeper underground. The microphone picks up distant dripping and a low mechanical hum that doesn’t sync with the image.

The end of the tunnel branches: one path goes down a set of stairs, the other toward a door marked “TRANSMISSION ROOM”.

TAPE B // CUT 03 CAM 09 // LOWER LEVEL

The camera stutters as it descends the stairs. At the bottom, the tunnel opens into a circular chamber. In the center sits an old TV on a metal cart, turned toward the camera, screen glowing dimly with static.

On the recording, you watch the camera walk up to the TV and place something on top of it: a tape with your archive ID written on it.

TAPE B // CUT 04 CAM 11 // TRANSMISSION ROOM

The tunnel ends at a heavy metal door. The sign reads: “LOCAL BROADCAST – AUTHORIZED PERSONNEL ONLY”. The handle turns by itself. The door opens.

Inside, racks of equipment blink weakly. A wall of small CRT screens shows different angles of the same empty room: your archive room. On one of the screens, the chair is empty. On another, someone is sitting in it, but the recording won’t focus on their face.

You Let It Play

On the screen, the camera presses the play button on the TV in the chamber. The static sharpens into a perfect copy of your own face, watching a screen. The speakers emit a long, flat tone.

Every monitor in the transmission room flips to the same channel. In the corner of each image, new text appears: “LIVE FEED – VIEWER: 01”.

Back in the archive room, the hum grows louder. Somewhere in town, all televisions briefly switch to a channel no one remembers tuning to. They see what you are seeing now.

END FILE // 02 STATUS: ARCHIVE SEALED
Ending: Door Left Closed

You Walk Away

You hit stop. The tape whirs, then falls silent. You remove it, put it back in its case, and slide the bolt on the archive door. The monitor stays black.

For a moment, you think you see a glow reflected in the glass, but when you look directly, it’s gone.

You make a note in the log: “TAPE A – UNVIEWABLE.” The entry writes itself a second time, slightly misaligned, as if the page is trying to remember where the line should be.

END FILE // 03 STATUS: LINE CONNECTED
Ending: The Call Is Coming From

You Look Too Closely

As you lean toward the monitor, the reflection in the car window sharpens. It’s the archive room, but older – different furniture, different equipment. The person in the chair turns toward the camera and lifts a phone receiver.

The phone on your own desk begins to ring. The number on the caller ID is your extension. The tape keeps playing, but the person on the screen is now waiting for you to answer.

You never hear your voice on the tape, but the recording doesn’t end.

END FILE // 04 STATUS: VIEWER NOT FOUND
Ending: Frame 0

You Eject the Tape

You slam the eject button. The tape shoots out of the VCR. The screen goes black, then shows a single freeze frame. It’s your chair, empty.

The timecode reads 00:00:00. The picture doesn’t change, no matter what you press.

In tomorrow’s logbook, someone writes that you never finished your shift. No one remembers when you left, only that your chair was still turning very slowly, as if someone had just stood up.

END FILE // 05 STATUS: LOOP DETECTED
Ending: Play Again

You Change the Channel

You rewind. You switch to Channel 0. You scrub frame by frame, searching for the moment the text on the mat changes, or the monitors sync up, or the face on the screen looks directly at you.

Eventually you find it: a frame you don’t remember seeing before. The caption reads: “SELECT NEXT CUT TO CONTINUE.”

The image on the tape is the exact moment you first sat down to watch these tapes. On the desk, two tapes are already halfway out of their cases: one marked A – HOUSE, the other B – SERVICE TUNNEL.

END FILE // 06 STATUS: STATIC ONLY
Ending: White Noise

You Pull the Plug

You reach down and yank the power cable from the wall. Every machine in the room dies at once. The sudden silence is louder than the static ever was.

You hold your breath. The monitor, disconnected and dark, flashes for a moment with a final message: “POWER LOST – RESUMING FROM LAST VIEWER.”

When the room is fully dark, you can still see the faint outline of your reflection on the glass, lit by a glow that isn’t there.
